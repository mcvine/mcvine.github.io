Incoherent inelastic neutron scattering -- Vanadium

Typical data

Scattering Cross Section

Incoherent inelastic neutron scattering spectra are easiest to interpret. The coherent neutron scattering length of Vanadium is far less than its incoherent neutron scattering length, making it an ideal example for illustrating incoherent inelastic scattering, and a standard calibration sample for neutron experiments.

The one-phonon incoherent inelastic neutron scattering cross section for a single-element material is given by

$$ \left( \frac{d^2\sigma}{d\Omega dE_f} \right) _{\pm 1} = \frac{\sigma_{inc}}{4\pi} \frac{k_f}{k_i} N \; \frac{\hbar^2Q^2}{2M} \exp(-2W) \frac{Z(E)}{E} \left \{ \coth \left( \frac{\hbar \omega}{2k_B T} \right) \pm 1 \right \}/2 $$

where $k_i$, $k_f$ is the momentum of the incident and outgoing neutron, respectively, $Q$ is the momentum transfer, $E$ is the energy transfer, $\left\{ \coth \left(\frac{\hbar \omega}{2k_B T} \right) \pm 1 \right\}/2$ is the thermal factor, and $Z(E)$ is the phonon density of states as a function of phonon energy $E$, which is the main input of the kernel.

Q-dependency

Let us take a more careful look at the various factors in this formula. It only depends on the phonon Density of States (DOS) of the sample. This property makes the spectra easy to understand and interpret.

The factor $2W$ is the Debye-Waller factor $$ 2W = \frac{\hbar^2 Q^2}{2M} \int \frac{1}{\hbar\omega} \coth(\frac{1}{2}\beta \hbar \omega) Z(\omega) d\omega $$

which can be rewritten as

$$ 2W = C_{DW} Q^2 $$ and $$ C_{DW} = \frac{\hbar^2}{2M} \int \frac{1}{\hbar\omega} \coth(\frac{1}{2}\beta \hbar \omega) Z(\omega) d\omega $$

The $Q$ dependency of the spectrum is solely determined by the term

$$ \frac{\hbar^2 Q^2}{2M} \exp(-C_{DW} Q^2) $$
